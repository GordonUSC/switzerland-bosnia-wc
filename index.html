The Setup

A four-way tie, and a six-pointer at SoFi

Group B could not be tighter. After one round, all four teams sit on a single point with an identical goal difference of zero — every game ended 1–1. Switzerland battered Qatar but were pegged back in stoppage time. Bosnia led Canada through 78 minutes before Cyle Larin's equalizer. Now the two of them meet with the group wide open, and a win all but books a place in the Round of 32.

The Road to SoFi

How Bosnia knocked out Italy to get here

Bosnia reached only their second-ever World Cup the hard way — through the March 2026 European playoffs, beating Wales and then four-time world champions Italy, both on penalties.

26 March 2026 · Playoff Semifinal · Cardiff
Wales 1–1 Bosnia 4–2 pens
Džeko scored, Daniel James equalized, and Bosnia held their nerve in the shootout in Wales.
31 March 2026 · Playoff Final · Zenica
Italy 1–1 Bosnia 4–1 pens
Moise Kean put Italy ahead before Alessandro Bastoni was sent off. Haris Tabaković equalized on 79'. In the shootout only Sandro Tonali scored for Italy — and 21-year-old Esmir Bajraktarević buried the winning penalty to send Italy out of a third straight World Cup.
12 June 2026 · World Cup · Toronto
Canada 1–1 Bosnia
Lukić's header gave Bosnia the lead; Larin levelled late. One point, and everything to play for at SoFi.

Why this one hits different in L.A.

A World Cup of diasporas, at SoFi

Both squads are stories of movement. Switzerland field one of the most multicultural teams in world football — Embolo born in Cameroon, Akanji of Nigerian heritage, Zakaria of Congolese descent, captain Xhaka of Kosovar-Albanian roots. Bosnia carry a nation rebuilt after war, with a Wisconsin-born hero in Bajraktarević and a 40-year-old captain who has scored in every corner of Europe.

In a city built by people from everywhere, two teams of people from everywhere play for a place in the knockout rounds. That's the World Cup the way it's supposed to feel.

The Verdict

What to expect

Switzerland have the better players in most areas of the pitch. The question is whether they finish.

The Nati out-shot Qatar 26 to 6 and still drew. That profligacy is the one crack in a side that is, by reputation, tactically mature: structured, hard to break down, and led by a captain in Xhaka who has seen everything. Expect them to dominate the ball through a midfield three and ask Ndoye and Vargas to stretch Bosnia's back four.

Bosnia will sit deeper and trust the counter and the set piece — Lukić's header against Canada was no accident, and Kolašinac and Demirović give them real presence on dead balls. They defend a lead well; they just have to defend it for longer than they managed in Toronto. And looming over it all is the bench, where a 40-year-old captain waits for the moment the game opens up.

A four-way tie, a wounded favorite, a romantic underdog who just eliminated Italy, and a Los Angeles crowd that will feel like everywhere at once. Whatever the scoreline, this is the World Cup doing exactly what it does best.
